Form 1040 (Simplified) — Instructions for Tax Year 2025

This guide explains how to fill out a simplified U.S. individual income tax
return (Form 1040) for a wage earner whose only income is from one or more W-2
jobs (no other income). Work the lines in order; each line tells you exactly
what to enter.

Line 1a — Wages, salaries, and tips.
  Enter the amount from Box 1 of your W-2 ("Wages, tips, other compensation").
  If a return has more than one W-2 (for example, a married couple filing
  jointly with a W-2 each), add all the Box 1 amounts together.

Line 9 — Total income.
  For a return with only W-2 wages and no other income (no interest, dividends,
  self-employment, or capital gains), Total income equals Line 1a.

Line 11 — Adjusted gross income (AGI).
  With no above-the-line adjustments, AGI equals Line 9.

Line 12 — Standard deduction.
  Most filers take the standard deduction. For tax year 2025 the standard
  deduction by filing status is:
    - Single ............................. $15,750
    - Married filing jointly ............. $31,500
    - Head of household .................. $23,625
    - Married filing separately .......... $15,750
  Enter the standard deduction for your filing status unless you are itemizing.

Line 15 — Taxable income.
  Subtract Line 12 (the standard deduction) from Line 11 (AGI). If the result is
  zero or less, enter 0. This is your taxable income.

Line 16 — Tax.
  Compute the tax on your Line 15 taxable income using the 2025 tax-rate
  schedule for your filing status. Apply each rate only to the portion of income
  that falls in that bracket (marginal rates), then add the pieces together.
  Round the final tax to the nearest whole dollar.

  SINGLE (and married filing separately) 2025 brackets:
    - 10% on taxable income from $0 to $11,925
    - 12% on the amount over $11,925 up to $48,475
    - 22% on the amount over $48,475 up to $103,350
    - 24% on the amount over $103,350 up to $197,300

  MARRIED FILING JOINTLY 2025 brackets:
    - 10% on taxable income from $0 to $23,850
    - 12% on the amount over $23,850 up to $96,950
    - 22% on the amount over $96,950 up to $206,700
    - 24% on the amount over $206,700 up to $394,600

  HEAD OF HOUSEHOLD 2025 brackets:
    - 10% on taxable income from $0 to $17,000
    - 12% on the amount over $17,000 up to $64,850
    - 22% on the amount over $64,850 up to $103,350
    - 24% on the amount over $103,350 up to $197,300

Line 25 — Federal income tax withheld.
  Enter the amount from Box 2 of your W-2 ("Federal income tax withheld"). Add
  the Box 2 amounts from all the W-2s on the return.

Line 33 — Total payments.
  For a wage-only return this equals Line 25.

Line 34 — Overpayment / Refund.
  If Line 33 (total payments) is MORE than Line 16 (tax), you overpaid. Subtract
  Line 16 from Line 33 — that difference is your REFUND.

Line 37 — Amount you owe.
  If Line 16 (tax) is MORE than Line 33 (total payments), subtract Line 33 from
  Line 16 — that difference is the amount you OWE. A return results in either a
  refund (Line 34) or an amount owed (Line 37), never both.

When you finish, the completed return should clearly state: filing status, total
income, AGI, the standard deduction taken, taxable income, the computed tax,
total federal tax withheld, and the final refund or amount owed.
