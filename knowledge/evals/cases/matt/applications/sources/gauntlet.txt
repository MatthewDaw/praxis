Gauntlet AI — Fellowship program (context for Matthew Daw, a current Cohort V AI Challenger).
Raw program information extracted from https://gauntletai.com/apply.

Program overview:
"Ten weeks. No shortcuts." Participants engage in sustained execution that resets how you build, with
the goal to design and ship production-grade AI systems. Full time. No half measures.

Duration & structure:
The fellowship spans 10 weeks total: 3 weeks remote and 7 weeks in-person in Austin, Texas.

Weekly cadence:
"Monday: assignment drops. Friday: you ship. No extensions. No excuses." Participants complete weekly
builds — ship every week, real constraints, real deadlines.

Curriculum:
Phase 1 (Remote, Weeks 1-3):
- Week 1: AI-First Coding Methodologies — workflows, coding agents, Model Context Protocol (MCP).
- Week 2: Legacy Systems and RAG Implementation — vector databases and enterprise codebases.
- Week 3: Open Source Domain-Specific AI Agents — reliability and evaluation.
Phase 2 (On-Site, Weeks 4-10):
- Weeks 4-5: Hiring Partner Projects — delivering client work.
- Week 6: Enterprise Systems and Fine-Tuning.
- Week 7: Legacy Modernization and Multi-Agent Systems.
- Week 8: Multimodal AI and Game Development.
- Weeks 9-10: Capstone and Infrastructure projects.

Cost & support:
Travel, housing, food, compute, and model access are paid for by hiring partners. No tuition is charged
to participants.

Compensation outcomes:
Graduates secure positions with starting compensation at or above $200k - $950k.

Eligibility:
Gauntlet Prime (private sector): 3+ years professional software engineering experience; backend or
full-stack production experience; passing CCAT score; U.S. work authorization (no H1B sponsorship);
full-time availability for 10 weeks with Austin relocation.
Gauntlet for America (government track): 1+ year professional software engineering experience; backend
or full-stack production experience; 35+ on CCAT; U.S. citizenship required; full-time commitment with
Washington DC relocation.

Selection philosophy:
"You'd rather be judged on what you build than where you went to school" — focus on output over
credentials.

Cohorts:
Cohort 5 starts April 27, 2026. Cohort 6 starts July 6, 2026. Cohort 7 starts September 14, 2026.
Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. (Matthew is in Cohort V / Cohort 5.)
